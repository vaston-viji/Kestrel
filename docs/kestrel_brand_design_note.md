# Kestrel Brand Design Note for Claude

## Brand intent
Design for a premium defence and defence-industry market scan aimed at senior internal audiences.
The tone should feel strategic, watchful and modern.
Do not make it look militaristic, aggressive or tactical.
Aim for a briefing identity, not a news masthead.

## Palette
Use the colour swatches as the master palette.

- **Graphite** `#4E4E51`: primary base colour for text, outlines and structure.
- **Violet** `#7766EC`: premium accent and hero brand colour.
- **Electric Blue** `#2272FF`: signal colour for urgency, intelligence and motion.
- **Lavender** `#BCA3EE`: soft support colour for secondary fills or subtle gradients.
- **Light Grey** `#D9D9D9`: background tint, separators and breathing space.

## Visual style
- Keep the mark clean, angular and aerodynamic.
- Favour hard edges, tapered forms and forward motion.
- Use negative space deliberately.
- Avoid glossy effects, bevels, drop shadows and military clichés.
- Prefer flat colour or very restrained gradient use.

## Typography
- Use a clean geometric or neo-grotesque sans serif.
- The main wordmark should be bold, tightly kerned and uppercase or title case.
- The sub-heading should be smaller, lighter and clearly subordinate.
- Prioritise legibility in Outlook and PowerPoint.

## Logo direction for **Kestrel**
- Use a stylised kestrel form, preferably head, wing or diving silhouette.
- The bird should imply precision, surveillance and speed.
- The icon should feel intelligence-led, not decorative.
- Use **Violet** and **Electric Blue** as the main logo colours.
- Set the wordmark in **Graphite**.
- Use the sub-heading exactly as: **D&DI market scan**

## Logo direction for **KPMG concept lock-up**
- This is a concept expression only, not a replacement for the official masterbrand.
- Retain the recognisable box motif in a simplified form.
- Use **Graphite** for the wordmark and structure.
- Introduce **Violet** and **Electric Blue** as premium accent fills or underline elements.
- Keep the result restrained and corporate.

## Layout guidance
- Leave generous whitespace around the mark.
- Prefer horizontal lock-ups for email headers.
- Keep icon and wordmark balanced, with the icon able to stand alone as a favicon or tile.
- Ensure the logo works on white, light grey and graphite backgrounds.

## Do
- Design for authority, clarity and speed.
- Make the identity feel Australian, strategic and current.
- Keep it simple enough to scale.

## Avoid
- camouflage motifs
- literal crosshairs or weapons references
- heavy gradients
- generic eagle imagery
- decorative shields unless highly simplified
