# Kestrel, brand rebuild guide

## What is in the approved logo

The approved logo shows:
- a **stylised kestrel head** emerging from a folded **envelope / chevron** device
- the wordmark **KESTREL** in a bold, uppercase geometric sans style
- the descriptor **DEFENCE INSIGHTS** in smaller, tracked uppercase text
- a cool palette built from **deep navy, indigo, violet and royal blue** on a pale neutral background

This identity reads as strategic, modern and signal-led rather than militarised.

## Core brand idea

Kestrel should signal three things fast:
1. **Observation**: sharp sight, scanning, intelligence
2. **Signal**: concise insight, market relevance, what matters now
3. **Professionalism**: credible enough for partners and senior internal audiences

## Colour system

Use these colours as the working palette.

- Background mist: `#EDECEF`
- Navy deep: `#2F364A`
- Navy mid: `#3A4152`
- Indigo: `#6451B3`
- Violet: `#8A6BCD`
- Royal blue: `#2568D0`
- Electric blue: `#2348AC`
- Ice: `#CDD0DF`
- White: `#FFFFFF`

### Usage logic
- **Navy deep** is the anchor colour for text and structure.
- **Indigo** and **violet** carry premium brand character.
- **Royal blue** and **electric blue** carry motion, signal and digital energy.
- **Background mist** keeps the system calm and premium.
- **Ice** is for subtle support on dark backgrounds.

## Logo anatomy

### Symbol
The symbol is the left-hand mark only:
- kestrel head
- envelope / folded chevron base
- internal facets in indigo, violet and blue

Use the symbol on its own only for:
- app tiles
- Teams or SharePoint icons
- favicons
- small avatar-style placements

### Full lock-up
The default production lock-up is:
- symbol on the left
- wordmark on the right
- descriptor below the wordmark

Use the full lock-up for:
- email mastheads
- PowerPoint covers
- PDF covers
- campaign tiles
- banner treatments

## Rebuild instructions for a designer or Claude

### 1. Use the supplied raster logo as the source of truth
Do not redraw the bird loosely. If a vector rebuild is required, trace the supplied symbol carefully and preserve:
- beak angle
- eye placement
- envelope fold proportions
- slope of the upper head shape
- internal facet relationships

### 2. Preserve typography hierarchy
- Wordmark: bold uppercase geometric sans feeling
- Descriptor: smaller uppercase, tracked out, centred under the wordmark block
- The descriptor should remain **DEFENCE INSIGHTS** unless a deliberate sub-brand is being created

### 3. Use generous clear space
Minimum clear space should equal at least the height of the letter **K** in the wordmark.
Do not crowd the mark with headlines or imagery.

### 4. Keep placements simple
Preferred background colours:
- `#EDECEF`
- white
- `#2F364A`

Avoid placing the mark over busy photography, hard gradients or complex illustrations.

### 5. Build a supporting pattern from the envelope geometry
The supporting graphics in this pack use angular chevrons and triangular facets derived from the envelope device.
This is the right visual language for extensions.

## Tone of voice

Kestrel should sound:
- sharp
- commercially aware
- future-oriented
- executive-ready
- concise

Kestrel should not sound:
- militarised
- breathless
- generic
- recruitment-marketing cheesy
