Priority Developments
01

Hunter-class structural design authority transferred to Australian sovereign engineering teams

What happened

BAE Systems confirmed at the Hunter-class Preliminary Design Review that structural design authority has formally transferred to Australian-based engineering teams operating under CASG — the first major sovereign capability handover under the program.

Why it matters

The transfer activates Australian workforce uplift requirements under the AUKUS Industrial Strategy and sets a contractual precedent for through-life sovereign support obligations — reducing single-supplier concentration risk at the design layer.

Kestrel Angle

Advisory firms embedded in CASG or Naval Group should expect accelerated programme assurance and workforce compliance demand as the design authority transfer is operationalised across the supply chain over the next 18 months.

02

ASD confirms expanded Cyber Shields initiative with $340M sovereign uplift allocation and 60% domestic workforce floor

What happened

The Australian Signals Directorate released updated Cyber Shields program timelines and confirmed a $340M sovereign industry investment tranche, with a new requirement mandating a minimum 60% domestic workforce on all protected workstreams.

Why it matters

The workforce mandate creates immediate supply pressure across the cyber advisory sector — domestic cleared talent is already constrained, and the new floor will intensify competition for accredited professionals across government and industry primes.

Kestrel Angle

Professional services firms advising ASD-adjacent clients should flag the 60% domestic floor as a near-term staffing constraint in all cyber assurance engagements; workforce sourcing and clearance uplift advisory will be a high-demand line through FY27.
